I am a second-year PhD student in the [Department of Agricultural and Applied Economics](https://aae.wisc.edu/) at the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

My research lies at the intersection of **land economics**, **environmental economics**, and **urban economics**. I study how environmental and agricultural conservation policies, groundwater regulation, and land use restrictions affect housing markets, property values, and local economic development. I am also interested in non-market valuation and sorting.

**Current ongoing projects include:**
- The effect of community-facing agricultural conservation efforts on residential property values in Wisconsin (with Jeffrey Hadachek and Wendong Zhang)
- Local economic consequences of groundwater regulation under SGMA (with Jeffrey Hadachek)
- A residential sorting model of experiential culinary diversity as an urban consumption amenity (with Peng Liu and Wendong Zhang)

Before coming to Wisconsin, I earned an M.S. in Applied Economics and Management from Cornell University and a B.S. in Economics from The Ohio State University.
